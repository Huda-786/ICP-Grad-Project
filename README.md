Customer service centers in multilingual and culturally diverse environments face significant communication challenges that directly impact service quality, operational efficiency, and overall customer satisfaction. In organizations such as the UAE’s Federal Authority for Identity, Citizenship, Customs & Port Security (ICP), where services like identity card issuance, biometric registration, and residency processing are performed daily, customers often experience delays due to language barriers, unfamiliarity with procedures, and difficulty understanding instructions. Employees are expected to handle customers of different nationalities and switch languages while adhering to strict and precise procedures.

Existing solutions such as information kiosks, printed brochures, and traditional queuing systems offer only limited support. These approaches lack personalization, interactivity, and flexibility in addressing diverse linguistic needs. Expanding human resources or hiring interpreters increases operational costs and is not scalable. Furthermore, most digital systems in such environments are designed primarily for transaction processing rather than customer guidance, resulting in fragmented and often confusing user experiences.

To address these challenges, this project proposes the development of an AI-powered interactive cultural-friendly persona designed for ICP service centers. The proposed solution is an intelligent AI agent deployed within the center to assist customers through multilingual interaction through a simple and intuitive interface. Upon arrival, customers select their language by clicking on their country flag displayed on the system interface. The AI persona appears in the national custom, then provides step-by-step guidance tailored to the selected ICP service, such as new ID issuance, renewal, replacement, or biometric updates.

Technically, the system is designed as a real-time conversational AI pipeline integrating a large language model (LLM), Gemini, with a Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) framework and a streaming communication layer powered by LiveKit enabling low-latency and natural interaction. User speech is captured and streamed in real time, converted into text using speech-to-text, and transformed into semantic embeddings. These embeddings query a vector database containing structured ICP service knowledge, where relevant information is retrieved using similarity measures such as cosine similarity. The retrieved context is injected into the LLM prompt, ensuring responses are grounded in official data and minimizing hallucinations. The backend, implemented in Python, orchestrates session handling, retrieval, and response generation, supporting multi-turn dialogue and dynamic intent recognition. Responses are converted into speech using text-to-speech and streamed back with low latency, while simultaneously rendered through an interactive avatar using HeyGen, providing synchronized visuals, captions, and guided interface elements.

The innovation of this system lies in combining multilingual adaptability, conversational intelligence, and process-specific guidance within a physical service environment. Unlike static systems, it delivers dynamic, personalized assistance based on user needs, significantly reducing dependency on frontline staff for routine inquiries, minimizing errors, and improving service speed.

The potential applications extend beyond ICP services to broader government and public service centers operating in multicultural contexts. By enhancing clarity, efficiency, and accessibility, it supports operational optimization while improving customer satisfaction. Ultimately, it bridges the communication gap between customers and service providers, creating a more inclusive, efficient, and intelligent service environment within ICP centers.
